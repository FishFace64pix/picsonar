## 5. Biometric Data and Facial Recognition Explained

PicSonar uses facial recognition technology to help event guests find photos of themselves. This section explains in plain language how this process works.

### How Facial Feature Vectors Work

When a guest takes a selfie, our system uses an AI model to analyze the geometry of the face — including distances between facial landmarks such as eyes, nose, and mouth — and converts these measurements into a numerical representation called a facial feature vector. This vector is a string of numbers that mathematically describes the face, but from which the original photograph cannot be reconstructed.

A similar extraction process is applied to faces found in uploaded event photos. The system then compares the guest's vector against indexed vectors to identify matching photos.

### Why This Constitutes Biometric Data

Under GDPR Article 4(14), biometric data means personal data resulting from specific technical processing relating to the physical characteristics of a natural person which allow or confirm the unique identification of that person. Facial feature vectors fall within this definition and are therefore classified as special category data under GDPR Article 9, subject to heightened protection.

### What We Do NOT Do

- We do not use biometric data for any purpose other than event photo matching.
- We do not sell, license, or share biometric data with third parties for commercial purposes.
- We do not create persistent biometric profiles of individuals.
- We do not use biometric data for identification beyond the specific event context.
- We do not store the raw selfie photograph after the feature vector has been extracted (unless required for dispute resolution, as described below).

---

## 1. What Is PicSonar Asking You to Do?

PicSonar allows you to find photos of yourself from this event using facial recognition technology. To use this service, you will be asked to take a selfie using your device's camera. The system will then compare your face with photos from this event and show you the photos that match.

This service is **entirely optional.** If you prefer not to use facial recognition, you will not be able to use the automated photo-finding feature. However, the event photographer may provide alternative ways to access event photos — please contact the event organizer for details.

## 2. What Biometric Data Is Processed?

When you take a selfie, the PicSonar system performs the following processing:

| Processing Step | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Step 1: Selfie Capture** | You take a photo of your face using your device camera. The photo is transmitted securely to PicSonar's servers over an encrypted connection. |
| **Step 2: Feature Vector Extraction** | An AI model analyzes the geometry of your face and converts it into a numerical representation called a facial feature vector. This is a string of numbers that describes the unique geometry of your face. |
| **Step 3: Photo Matching** | Your facial feature vector is compared against facial feature vectors that have been pre-extracted from the event's uploaded photos. Photos with matching vectors are returned to you. |
| **Step 4: Deletion** | Your facial feature vector is deleted automatically when the event expires. Your selfie photograph is deleted immediately after the feature vector is extracted. |

## 3. What Is a Facial Feature Vector?

A facial feature vector is a mathematical description of your face — essentially a list of numbers that captures the distances and proportions between facial landmarks (such as the distance between your eyes, the shape of your nose, and the contour of your jaw).

This vector is **not a photograph** and cannot be reverse-engineered into a photograph of you. However, under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), facial feature vectors are classified as **biometric data** — a special category of personal data that receives the highest level of legal protection.

> **Important:** Your facial feature vector is stored in encrypted form and is used only to find your photos at this event. It is not used to identify you in other contexts, track your movements, or shared with any third parties for commercial purposes.
